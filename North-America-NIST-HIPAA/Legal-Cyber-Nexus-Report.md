Part A: The Simulated Issue (Legal Compliance Crisis)
The Scenario: A Vancouver-based law firm, "Wozney & Associates," representing high-profile medical malpractice clients, discovers an unauthorized data export from their cloud storage.

The Twist: The data contains both legal case files and HIPAA-protected health records.

The Conflict: Standard IT sees a "data leak," but a Specialist sees a Legal Ethics & HIPAA Breach nightmare.

Part B: The Unique Portfolio Report (Copy & Paste)
Report Title: The Legal-Cyber Nexus: Forensic Audit of Unauthorized ePHI Exfiltration

Specialist: Melissa Rachel Wozney, GRC Specialist

Regulatory Focus: HIPAA Security Rule & Law Society of BC Data Standards

1. Contextual Identification (The Legal Lens)

Discovery: Detected 2.5GB of data moving to an unrecognized IP address in Eastern Europe.

Sensitive Assets: Litigation strategy documents and client medical histories. This creates a double liability: Attorney-Client Privilege and HIPAA Compliance.

2. Strategic Mitigation (NIST CSF 2.0 GV.SC)

Action: Triggered "Kill-Switch" on all remote access tokens.

Legal Hold: Implemented a formal forensic hold on all server logs to ensure evidence is admissible in court—a step often missed by non-legal IT staff.

3. The Compliance Cross-Walk (The Melissa Method)

HIPAA §164.308: Verified that "Unique User Identification" was active, allowing us to trace the leak to a compromised credential.

Legal Ethics: Prepared a brief for the Partners on their ethical duty to notify clients under the Model Rules of Professional Conduct.

4. Post-Mortem: The Psychology of the "Insider"

Finding: The breach was a "Social Engineering" success. The attacker posed as a Judge’s clerk.

The Fix: Developed a specialized training module for legal assistants on verifying digital identities in high-pressure litigation environments.
