Report Title: Ethical Privacy Audit: Mitigating Behavioral Risks in Fintech

Specialist: Melissa Rachel Wozney, GRC Specialist

Regulatory Focus: Singapore PDPA (Personal Data Protection Act) & APRA CPS 234

1. Behavioral Audit (The Psychology Lens)

The Issue: Identified "Deceptive Design" in the app interface that pressured users to "Agree All" without reading. This violates the PDPA principle of Reasonable Consent.

Finding: High user anxiety and low trust scores were directly linked to opaque data collection practices.

2. Technical Remediation (NIST CSF 2.0 PR.DS)

The Fix: Implemented Granular Consent. Users can now opt-in to "Payments" but opt-out of "Marketing" without losing app functionality.

Data Minimization: Purged all non-essential contact data collected during the "Dark Pattern" phase to reduce the risk of a high-impact breach.

3. Financial Resilience (APRA CPS 234)

Cross-Border Audit: Verified that all Australian user data held by Lumina Pay is encrypted at the same standard as local Singaporean data, ensuring compliance for potential expansion into the Australian market.

4. Post-Mortem: The "Human Firewall"

Result: Increased user retention by 12% after moving to a "Transparency-First" model.

The Lesson: In the APAC region, Privacy = Trust. When you respect the user’s psychology, you secure the business’s future.
